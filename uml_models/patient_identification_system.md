# Patient Identification System

The Patient Identification System ensures that each incoming cardiovascular measurement is linked to the correct hospital patient before the rest of CHMS can use it. This is a high-risk subsystem because an incorrect identity match could attach alerts or vital signs to the wrong person. The design therefore separates matching, record retrieval, authorization, and anomaly handling into distinct responsibilities.

`PatientIdentifier` performs the actual matching logic. It validates the incoming `IdentifierToken`, compares the supplied `DemographicProfile` with candidate records, and produces a `MatchResult` with an explicit confidence score. `HospitalPatient` represents the hospital-side patient record and contains only the identity and clinical summary needed by this subsystem. Retrieval of those records is delegated to `PatientRepository`, which keeps database-style lookup concerns out of the matching class.

`IdentityManager` is the coordinating service. It requests candidate patients from the repository, invokes `PatientIdentifier`, and decides what to do with the result. If there is no match, or if the result is ambiguous or inconsistent, the manager records an `IdentityAnomaly` for review rather than allowing the data to pass silently. This makes mismatch handling explicit and preserves data integrity by preventing unsafe patient associations.

Privacy is enforced through `AccessPolicy`. Even after a patient is identified, patient details are released only if the requester role is permitted to view them; otherwise, masked information is returned. This limits unnecessary exposure of names and medical history. Overall, the model is modular and maintainable because each class has a focused role: matching, patient record access, orchestration, authorization, and anomaly management can all evolve independently without turning the subsystem into a single overburdened class.
