# Data Storage System

The Data Storage System is responsible for preserving cardiovascular observations so they can support both immediate clinical review and later historical analysis. `DataStorage` is the subsystem boundary. It stores records in a controlled repository, keeps audit evidence, and applies retention rules rather than exposing low-level persistence logic to the rest of CHMS. This keeps storage concerns separate from alerting and patient identification.

`PatientData` models one vital-sign record at one moment in time. It therefore carries the patient identifier, metric type, measured value, unit, and the timestamp at which the observation was recorded. Historical lookup is supported by querying these timestamped records over a time window. Because clinical data may be corrected after validation, each `PatientData` object maintains a version history through composition with `DataVersion`. That class records the revised value, who made the correction, when it happened, and why, which supports traceability and clinical accountability.

`DataRetriever` exists so that read access is handled separately from storage. A clinician or other requester submits an `AccessRequest`, and `AccessController` checks whether that role and purpose of use are allowed to retrieve the requested patient data. The filtering operation makes least-privilege access explicit: users receive only the fields appropriate for their role instead of unrestricted access to all stored data.

Deletion and long-term governance are modeled by `RetentionPolicy`. It decides whether old records should be archived first or deleted after the retention period, while also respecting legal hold cases. `AuditEntry` records access events for security review. Overall, the design is modular because storage, querying, authorization, version tracking, and lifecycle management are handled by distinct classes with clear responsibilities, making the subsystem easier to maintain and extend.
